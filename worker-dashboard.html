📌 Always Remember
🌵 DRY SEASON WATER ALERT
November to March — fresh water must be checked and refilled every single day. This is a mandatory logged task. A nursing or pregnant female without water is an emergency.
🤰Watch for pregnancy signs — visible belly from Month 3, weight gain, nesting behaviour. Log the expected birth date immediately.
🍼Birth occurred? 72-hour no-disturb protocol. Do not touch pups. Double feed and water for the nursing female — she is feeding herself and 5–8 hybrid pups. Log pup count at birth and at 24 hours.
♂️Hybrid growing males must be separated by 12 weeks — move them at 10 weeks before fighting starts. Hybrid males are larger and stronger — their fights kill. One male per grow-out slot, no exceptions.
🐕No dogs or cats within 5 metres of any cage. Their scent alone causes panic and stress in the whole colony — even if the animals never see them.
🔇Move slowly, speak quietly near maternity cages always. Loud noise or sudden movement near a new mother can cause her to harm her pups.
🔴Any RED health status — notify the owner the same day. Do not wait for the weekly report on Sunday.

🐾 Log a Colony Event
Only log when something actually happens — a birth, death, isolation, male movement, maternity entry, weaning, or backdated entry request.
All animals on this farm are hybrid. No breed field needed.
Select the founding female who just gave birth. Her GC ID starts with GC-F (founder). Example: GC-F-002 = Batch A Female 1. The system will auto-generate the Litter ID from her ID and litter count.
The Litter ID is created automatically from the mother's GC ID and her litter count. Format: LIT-[mother GC ID]-[litter number]. Example: LIT-GC-F-002-01 means "first litter from GC-F-002". All pups from this birth share this ID until individually tagged at 10–12 weeks.
Take a photo of the animal immediately. If disease is suspected, notify the owner TODAY — do not wait for the weekly report. Submit one entry per animal.
Select the GC ID of the animal that died. GC-F = founding animal. GC-25/GC-26 = born on this farm. If the animal was a pup not yet individually tagged, select its Litter ID group instead and describe which pup in the notes.
Use this after a founding female dies and a replacement is purchased. The replacement gets the next available GC ID. Full 2-week quarantine applies before she joins the colony.
Which role is being filled? Example: BA-F1 slot is empty after a founding female died. The new animal gets a new GC ID and takes over the BA-F1 slot position.
Mark an animal as isolated (sick, aggressive, post-birth recovery). Owner sees this flagged immediately.
Select the GC ID of the animal you are isolating or returning to the colony. Isolation means moving the animal away from all others until it is fully healthy for 72+ hours.
Use this every time the breeding male moves from one batch to another. The system will calculate his expected removal date and the expected birth date for the batch he just entered.
Log when a pregnant female moves into a maternity cage. She stays until pups are 6 weeks old. Grease the cage hinges before she enters — silent door operation is required.
Select the GC ID of the pregnant female. Founding females are GC-F-002 through GC-F-005. The system links this maternity entry to her full breeding history automatically.
Log when pups are weaned at 6 weeks and moved from the maternity cage to the weaning cage. Male and female pups must be separated within the weaning cage before 10 weeks.
Select the mother whose pups are being weaned. The system links this weaning event to her litter record and updates her pup survival count.
The Litter ID of the pups being weaned. This was auto-generated when the birth was logged. Select it here to link this weaning event to the correct litter record.
Log when a growing male is moved into an individual grow-out slot. This is when his GC ID is assigned and the physical leg band goes on. Must happen before 12 weeks. One male per slot — no exceptions.
Select the Litter ID this pup belongs to. The system will assign the next available GC ID from that litter's reserved block and record the lineage (mother + father).

🏷️ Animal ID System — Read This First
Every animal on this farm has a permanent ID. This ID never changes, even if the animal moves cages or changes role. Always use the ID when logging events — never describe an animal by colour or size alone.
How to Read an ID
GC-F-001
GC = Grasscutter (every animal on this farm) F = Founder — this animal was NOT born on this farm. It came with the original colony on Day 1. 001 = Registration number. First animal registered = 001, second = 002, and so on.
GC-25-001
GC = Grasscutter 25 = Year of birth (2025). Born in 2026 = GC-26-001. 001 = First animal born on this farm in that year.
LIT-GC-F-002-01
LIT = Litter (a group of pups born together) GC-F-002 = The mother's ID 01 = This is her first litter. Second litter = 02, and so on. All pups from the same birth share this Litter ID until they are individually tagged at 10–12 weeks.
Slot Labels — Where an Animal Lives
Slot labels (BA-F1, BB-F2, G-07, MH, M1 etc.) describe where an animal currently is, not who it is. A slot label can change — if a female moves to a maternity cage, her slot changes but her GC ID stays the same forever.

Always use the GC ID when logging. Use the slot label only to describe location.
Physical Leg Band Rule
Pups cannot be physically tagged at birth — they are too small. The physical leg band goes on at 10–12 weeks, when the growing male is moved to his individual grow-out slot. That is the exact moment the GC ID becomes fully active.

Before 10 weeks → pups are tracked as a group under the Litter ID.
At 10–12 weeks → individual GC ID assigned, physical band applied, full individual tracking begins.
